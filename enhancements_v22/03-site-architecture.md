# Site Architecture

## Recommended top-level structure

Use a tighter and more premium information architecture than the current public navigation.

## Primary navigation

- Services
- Process
- Results
- About
- CTA: Book a Workflow Review

## Secondary pages

- Workflow Automation
- AI Adoption
- Operational Efficiency
- Book a Call
- Blog

## Recommended page purposes

### Home

Purpose:
- establish the positioning fast
- prove credibility
- explain the service lines
- create demand for the review call

### Services

Purpose:
- give buyers one overview page
- help them self-select the right service line
- explain how work is scoped and delivered

### Workflow Automation

Purpose:
- capture buyers with a clear automation problem
- explain what gets automated and how

### AI Adoption

Purpose:
- capture buyers who already have AI tools but lack usage and operating discipline

### Operational Efficiency

Purpose:
- position Hopfner above tactical automation only
- show the broader operating model and throughput lens

### Process

Purpose:
- remove uncertainty about how engagement works
- show structure and rigor

### Results

Purpose:
- provide proof
- house representative wins and later real case studies

### About

Purpose:
- explain the practice and working philosophy
- increase trust without bloated founder storytelling

### Book a Call

Purpose:
- convert demand already created elsewhere on the site
- make the first conversation feel specific and high-value

## Section strategy by page

Existing section types are enough to create a strong first version using:
- `nav_links`
- `hero_cta`
- `social_proof_strip`
- `card_grid`
- `title_body_list`
- `steps_list`
- `rich_text_block`
- `label_value_list`
- `proof_cluster`
- `case_study_split`
- `faq_list`
- `cta_block`
- `footer_grid`

## Pages to de-emphasize

- `Contact` should not be a primary page if `Book a Call` is the real conversion path.
- `Blog` should remain secondary until it has enough strong content to support authority.

## Global conversion strategy

Keep one main CTA language across the site:

`Book a Workflow Review`

This is more specific and premium than generic call-booking language.
